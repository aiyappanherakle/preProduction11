The U.S Gold First Spouse Series (2007 - Present)
In 2007, the U.S. Mint released the first coins in the First Spouse series. Each coin, issued in proof and business strike (Mint State) weighs half an oz. of gold with a denomination of $10.

When the coins were released in 2007, large quantities were sold-as evidenced by the final mintages listed below. Then by 2008 and more recent years (such as 2011) the amount of coins sold make these coins some of the lowest mintages of any U.S. coin.

Coins graded First Strike MS-70 or First Strike Proof-70 by PCGS sell for varying premiums-in some cases coins can be worth double with the First Strike designation (First Strike denotes that PCGS graded coins within the first 30 days of issue, or they were sent to PCGS in U.S. Mint shipping packages dated within 30 days of issue).

Gold Spouse coins are regularly featured in GreatCollections coin auctions and for direct sale in our Buy Now section.

View the Gold Spouse Coins at GreatCollections

The First Spouse Gold Coin obverses (heads sides) feature portraits of the first spouses, their names, the order and years of their term as first spouse, the year of minting or issuance, IN GOD WE TRUST and LIBERTY. Each coin has a unique reverse (tails) design featuring an image emblematic of that spouse's life and work, as well as the inscriptions UNITED STATES of AMERICA, E PLURIBUS UNUM, $10, 1/2 OZ. and .9999 FINE GOLD.

When a President served without a first spouse, a gold coin is issued bearing an obverse image emblematic of Liberty as depicted on a circulating coin of that era and a reverse image emblematic of a theme of that President's life. The coin is inscribed with the years and order of that President's term.
